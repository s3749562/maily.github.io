Project Ideas

Overview

My IT Project idea is developing a stock prediction system. Particularly, the system will gather, update and analyse the text of the related online news, then evaluate and make predictions on what stocks would drop or rise in what degree, by utilizing the Natural Language Processing and Machine Learning techniques.

This stock prediction system is considered as a stock adviser that provides investors more information and analysis, leading to the most secure and profitable decisions. Furthermore, the system reduces the amount of time comprehending and keeping up with the news which has a huge impact on the stock prices.

Therefore, the system could be launched as a mobile application and a website, allowing investors to access the system whenever and wherever they want.

Motivation

According to Hong (2020), up to 90% of the investors lose their money on the stock market. The top reasons are that people are not conducting enough online research; and diversify their portfolio with different industries and categories (Kunsman 2019).

  • Investors don't have enough time and knowledge to analyse online news

    • To invest in different industries or categories, Investors must deeply understand the characteristics of not only the company they are investing in, but also the overall industry. This includes interpreting the financial reports, keeping track of the stock chart and reading online news related to the company. Thus, it requires a large amount of time and manual analysis to invest in different companies.

    Once the online research has been conducted, it is still extremely time-consuming and costly to correctly evaluate how the piece of news would affect the stock prices. Due to the fact that the investors do not have experience or knowledge in an industry, they must spend time learning or hire an expert if they want to invest in such an industry.

  • News affects stock prices

    • Negative news such as bad rumors, political uncertainty and lapse in corporate governance cause people to sell stocks and decreases in prices, meanwhile, positive news like new product launch, corporate acquisitions and positive economy translate into buying pressure and increases in stock prices (Beers 2020). From that, reading and keeping up with the news online is one of the important tasks that informs investors to sell or buy stocks at the right time.

    The lack of time and knowledge for online research, together with the huge impact of online news and journals to stock prices, the majority of investors are not able to generate enough revenue from the stock market. Therefore, my stock prediction system will reduce the workload, save time and money, as well as provide the best analysis and advice for investors to decide; by analyzing online documents for them.

    Description

  • Gather, update and select the appropriate pieces of online news

    • Utilizing the techniques of Natural Language Processing (NLP), the system will identify and understand pieces of online news and journals around the world that contain relevant keywords and phrases which might affect the stock prices. If the content language is foreign, the system could also translate it into Vietnamese.

    The system will collaborate and be linked with global online newspapers and journalism brands such as Viet Nam News, Tuoi Tre News, Forbes and The New York Times, in order to showcase the most up-to-date and related news.

    For example, when an online article is released in Tuoi Tre News, the system will skim the texts and see if words and phrases like “drinks”, “Coca Cola”, “revenue declines” and “customers stop drinking” appear multiple times in the appropriate context. It will automatically understand that this piece of news is relevant to investors and could possibly affect the stock prices of Coca Cola company, the carbonated drinks category as well as the FMCG industry. Thus, showcasing such pieces of news on the platforms of the system for the investors to read.

  • Give predictions on what and how stocks will behave

    • The texts of the online newspapers and journals will be analyzed and compared with its data and learning experience, then make detailed predictions about what stocks will go up or down at what degree and at what time, using the Machine Learning techniques. Thus, advise investors to act immediately. This feature could be continuously improved by allowing the system users and developers to provide feedback. This is because the more data it has, the higher accuracy of the predictions.

    For instance, taking the mentioned example, after the relevant online article has been identified, the system would conclude that “Coca Cola company’s stock price is expected to significantly drop due to declining customer demand. Conversely, this could be an opportunity for its competitors like Pepsi to lead the market. Thus, investors should sell Coca Cola’s stock and buy Pepsi’s stock.”. The reason why the system is able to perform this prediction is because it has been “taught” that, if the words like “Coca Cola” and “customer stop drinking” appear in the same text, the outcome would be a decline in Coca Cola’s stock price; or the system has received many negative feedbacks when it says the stock price would increase, so it learned from past mistakes.

    However, this is just a very simple circumstance in which the investors could interpret the article themselves. In other complicated situations when the investors do not acquire the knowledge in a specific industry or category, this is when this system solves the problem.

    For example, when investors see an article that contains words like “oil company”, “loss”, “negative cash flow”, they could misunderstand that this oil company is in trouble and going to bankrupt and sell stocks immediately. Contrastly, experts in such an industry consider this article as an opportunity to buy stocks. This is because they understand the unique characteristics of the oil industry is that companies must invest in repairing and buying new equipment periodically, which costs a lot of money and completely normal, consequently, the temporary loss in revenue and the potential profit in the future.

    Therefore, my system works with experts in various industries as well as receives feedback from the knowledgeable users, to feed the system relevant knowledge and data to increase the prediction accuracy.

    IT Technologies

    Natural Language Processing

    What does it do?

    NLP refers to a form of Artificial Intelligence (AI) that enables computers to read, comprehend and interpret the appropriate meanings of human language (Poelmans 2020). In particular, NLP works by breaking down the language into shorter words and phrases, then recognizing the rules, relationships and patterns between them; thus creating the meanings (Anirudh 2020). The basic tasks of NLP includes part-of-speech tagging, lemmatization, tokenization, disambiguation, and semantics (Poelmans 2020). Moreover, since the text- and voice-based data from human language is extremely complex and diverse, NLP requires many techniques from statistical and machine learning to rules-based and algorithmic approaches, to interpret (Poelmans 2020).

    In the present day, there are a large number of NLP applications that we are using in our daily life, namely:

  • Machine translation: automatic translation of text or speech from one language to another (Google Translate).
  • Spell checking: recognizes spelling errors in the content and suggests it with a correct spelling (Grammarly, Microsoft Word and Google Docs).
  • Sentiment analysis: identify if the text shows negative or positive opinions, broadly used in Marketing to measure customer satisfaction (KeyHole and Brand 24).
  • Question replying: answers the questions raised by humans in both text and verbal (chatbots, Siri, Alexa and OK Google).

    • According to Anirudh (2020), NLP is expected to be more user-oriented in the future, due to the growth of technologies. Specifically, NLP applications will not just be restricted to resolve customer questions, but will also be able to provide greater technological help. For example, if someone asks NLP, “What is wrong with my network?”, it will not only be able to explain the problem, but also be aware of the user’s actual intention which is that he wants to access the network immediately.

    What is likely the impact?

    Potentially, NLP will be more and more useful for our lives, most importantly in terms of social. It changes the ways we communicate to achieve what we want because we no longer need another person or do things. Now, it can be easily seen that everyone talks to their Siri or Google to get tasks done or questions answered. Plus, virtual assistants and chatbots are able to do much more effective tasks than humans. Hence, allowing NLP users to have a more convenient life as well as a more productive business. This innovation is currently and expected to keep replacing people working in customer services and sales, such as salespeople in the next 20 years (King 2016).

    Machine Learning

    What does it do?

    Machine Learning (ML) is defined as an application of AI that provides systems the ability to automatically learn and improve from experience without being programmed to do so (Expert System Team 2020). Particularly, the learning process starts with observations or data such as examples, direct experience or instruction, in order to recognize patterns in data and make the most accurate predictions in the future (Expert System Team 2020). Additionally, the base of Machine Learning is computational mathematics and statistics (Pioneer Labs n.d.).

    There are three types of machine learning algorithms, namely (Pioneer Labs n.d.):

  • Supervised learning

    • After being provided with a number of input variables (x) and output variables (y), the machine will be able to make predictions about the output for any new given inputs. The most common examples are Linear regression and Nearest Neighbours.

  • Unsupervised learning

    • Provide the machine with input variables (x) but no output variables (y), to discover the underlying structure or distribution of data and help users make decisions. Some of the examples are the Apriori algorithm and k-means.

  • Reinforcement learning

    • The machine learns and trains itself continuously by receiving a positive or a negative feedback based on its actions. Some popular examples include Q-Learning and Temporal Difference.

    Today, Machine Learning has been applied in various tasks which helps us to make better decisions and process information faster. For instance, face detection, movies or product recommendation, fraud detection, predictions and so much more.

    In the future, the field of Machine Learning is likely to enhance its personalization aspects as well as increase the use of robots (Rose 2020). Firstly, by being able to satisfy customers with personalized experience, many businesses are aiming to improve personalization through ML techniques. Secondly, since robots perform tasks much faster and more accurate by ML algorithms, more businesses are adopting robots to increase productivity.

    What is likely the impact?

    Thanks to Machine Learning, human life could be positively impacted in a large number of ways in the future. For example, although self-driving cars still require at least one driver for safety, it is possible that we will have completely automated cars using the fundamentals of ML (Elite n.d.). This innovation promises a range of potential benefits for car users including, higher road safety (drunk people still able to use the car), increase independence (disable people or the elders still can use the car), more productivity (you could read the emails while the car drives itself) and environment-friendly (reduce fuel consumption). However, people working in transportation services like taxi drivers or delivery guys could potentially lose their jobs due to this potential product.
